In this presentation participants will:

  • Understand the Duluth Model of the “Power & Control Wheel”
  • Understand the common tactics of abuse
  • Be able to better assess and disengage from potentially abuse people and environments.
  • Relate tactics of abuse in Domestic Violence to other forms of oppression like racism, sexism, and classism.
  • Understand the psychological consequences of tactics described above (including internalized oppression)
  • Become introduced to shame’s role in perpetuating oppression
  • Understand how to implement specific empowerment tools to help reverse the effects of oppression
  • Refer to the “Wheel of Equality” as inspiration for how to overcome Domestic violence and other form of abuse and oppression.

In this presentation participants will:

  • Assess the current quality of relationship with themselves
  • Explore the fundamental requirements for a healthy relationship (with others and self)
  • Explore the role of self-talk in determining self-worth
  • Differentiate between “rumination” and “worrying”
  • Identify the common cognitive distortions
  • Learn how to revise negative self-talk
  • Explore the benefits of “Self-Mastery” as a concept and life style
  • Learn how to implement specific tools for self-mastery including controlling your focus.

In this presentation participants will:

  • Understand the relationship between beliefs, thoughts, emotions, and behavior.
  • Understand how beliefs are formed in the context of trauma
  • Recognize the common limiting beliefs for survivors of abuse
  • Recognize the emotional, and mental consequences of these common limiting beliefs
  • Explore their personal major limiting beliefs
  • Gain an understanding of how these beliefs have created consequences in different life areas
  • Learn how to revise limiting beliefs through changing state, focus, and other tools
  • Create a plan for addressing personal limiting beliefs

In this presentation participants will:

  • Gain a basic understanding of Domestic Violence (DV)
  • Gain a basic understanding of shame (including being introduced to research from Brene Brown)
  • Understand the common myths associated with DV and their role in collective and individual shame
  • Understand the role of systems in perpetuating these myths
  • Gain a basic understanding of internalized oppression
  • Become familiar with shame resiliency techniques for domestic violence survivors.
  • Understand a handful of ways to address shame in DV survivors and the community at large
