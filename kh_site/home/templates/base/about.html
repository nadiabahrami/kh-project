About Kyeisha

A Little About Me

I was born and raised in NYC surrounded by the electric energy, creativity, and bustling life of the city. I am passionate about healing individuals and groups affected by personal and collective trauma. I am often told that I invite authenticity into my interactions, challenge others in a way that is disarming but not overwhelming, a natural healer, and someone that brings laughter/playfulness, and creativity into the spaces I frequent.

I am also a dancer who is a huge fan of Beyonce’s choreography. Enough to learn her choreography and offer a dance class at a local dance studio! I also make a habit of indulging in the humor of life by finding ways to stay in contact with the joy and beauty that’s around me.

My Clinical & Personal Values

Self-Empowerment

I believe that we all have the right to make our own choices and the power to control our own lives. Personal and collective trauma often robs us of this birthright, I seek to restore this.

Equality

I operate from the basic assumption that we are equal collaborators. Experts in what we know and ignorant in what we do not. This framework establishes curiosity and partnership.

Transformation

I work from the idea that true transformation begins with the quality of our beliefs. Transforming our beliefs to live more authenticity and abundantly is true freedom.

Social Justice

My work challenges the emotional, mental, and psychological consequences of injustice in order to create social justice first internally then externally.

Thriving

All of my services help my clients to pursue a life where thriving isn’t just a possibility but a regular expectation.

Education and Training

I am a Licensed Mental Health Counselor (# MC 60507339) in Washington State. I graduated with honors with my Master’s Degree in Mental Health Counseling from CUNY Hunter College and did my undergraduate work in Psychology and Sociology at Stony Brook University.

I have worked in the mental health field at several outpatient treatment centers for those with chemical dependency issues and trauma. In 2012 I was licensed by New York State Department of Health (DOH) as a Rape Crisis Emergency Room Counselor. As of 2014, I received the training for Washington State requirements for a Domestic Violence direct service provider (WAC 388-61A-0350). I have also worked at the YWCA Domestic Violence Emergency Shelter where I assisted women fleeing from abusive relationships. In addition to this I have served as a mental health counselor at a low-income clinic for women called Seattle Therapy Alliance.

Anti-Oppression Work

I am committed to creating and maintaining opportunities for social justice and combating oppression. Below are some of my past work and involvements.

  • Extensive training from Seattle Therapy Alliance in social justice counseling and various issues related to the intersection of class, gender, race, and sexual orientation, and its impact on mental health.

  • Creating and facilitating the group “Having the Conversation: Discussing Race, Oppression and Privilege”. For almost a year, this free community group served participants as they navigated past and recent racism.

  • Served as a guest facilitator at the University of Washington’s (UW) “Racial Justice Organizing and Caucus” where I assisted in facilitating the conversation racism and various systems of oppression.

  • Provided psychosocial assessments to assist undocumented immigrants and their families.
